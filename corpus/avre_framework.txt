AGENTIC VALUE REALIZATION ENGINE (AVRE™) — FRAMEWORK REFERENCE DOCUMENT
Version 1.5 | Agni Sivaraman | Enterprise AI Governance

OVERVIEW
AVRE™ (Agentic Value Realization Engine) is a multi-lens value assessment framework designed to evaluate the true enterprise value of agentifying a business use case. It moves beyond simple ROI calculations to capture return on effort (ROE), return on freedom (ROF — strategic optionality created), and the risk-adjusted net value after applying ARM™ risk penalties.

AVRE™ CORE EQUATION
Net Enterprise Value (NEV) = Benefit Realization − (Capability Debt × ARM™ Risk Multiplier)

Where:
- Benefit Realization = Σ(ROI Score × 0.40) + Σ(ROE Score × 0.30) + Σ(ROF Score × 0.30)
- Capability Debt = CDR score from ARM™ assessment (0-10 scale)
- ARM™ Risk Multiplier = 1 + (ARM™ Composite Score / 10)

AVRE™ VALUE LENSES

1. ROI (Return on Investment)
Definition: Quantifiable financial or efficiency return from deploying the agent, measured in cost savings, revenue impact, or FTE hour recapture.
Assessment Dimensions:
- Time savings: Hours per week recaptured per user × FTE cost × scale
- Error reduction: Cost of current errors × reduction rate
- Throughput increase: Volume uplift × unit value
- Decision speed improvement: Cycle time reduction × business impact per cycle
Scoring:
- 1-3: Marginal ROI. Savings under 10% of current process cost. Hard to justify standalone.
- 4-6: Moderate ROI. 10-40% savings. Positive business case with clear payback within 18 months.
- 7-9: Strong ROI. >40% savings or significant revenue impact. Payback within 6-12 months.
- 10: Transformational. Order-of-magnitude improvement. New revenue streams or market position.

2. ROE (Return on Effort)
Definition: The organizational effort — human capital, change management, integration work — required to realize the ROI. High ROI with very high ROE may not be worth pursuing relative to alternatives.
Assessment Dimensions:
- Implementation complexity: Number of systems to integrate, data pipelines to build, stakeholders to align
- Change management load: Degree of workflow disruption, retraining needed, resistance risk
- Maintenance burden: Ongoing model monitoring, prompt management, escalation handling
- Time to first value: Weeks from kickoff to working prototype delivering real output
Scoring:
- 1-3: Very high effort. Complex integration, significant change management, long time to value. ROE score penalizes overall AVRE.
- 4-6: Moderate effort. Standard integration, manageable change management. Neutral ROE impact.
- 7-9: Low effort. Simple integration, quick wins, low disruption. ROE score amplifies overall AVRE.
- 10: Turnkey. Plug-and-play deployment with immediate value, minimal disruption.

3. ROF (Return on Freedom — Strategic Optionality)
Definition: The strategic value created beyond the immediate use case: platforms built, capabilities unlocked, competitive position strengthened, or future AI investments enabled.
Assessment Dimensions:
- Platform effect: Does this agent create reusable infrastructure (RAG corpus, tool integrations, agent patterns) that accelerates future deployments?
- Competitive moat: Does the agent create differentiation that competitors cannot easily replicate?
- Regulatory optionality: Does this deployment generate governance artifacts (audit trails, bias reports, conformity assessments) that simplify future high-risk AI deployments?
- Talent development: Does building this agent develop internal AI capability that compounds over time?
Scoring:
- 1-3: Isolated use case. No platform effect. Tactical value only.
- 4-6: Moderate optionality. Some reusable components. Builds one capability area.
- 7-9: Strong platform effect. Reusable across multiple future use cases. Builds significant organizational AI muscle.
- 10: Foundational. This agent is an AI platform — it enables an entire class of future deployments.

AVRE™ OPPORTUNITY COST DIMENSION
Beyond the three value lenses, AVRE™ explicitly scores opportunity cost — what the organization loses by NOT agentifying this use case.
- Score 1-3: Low opportunity cost. Manual process is adequate. Competitors are not significantly ahead.
- Score 4-6: Moderate opportunity cost. Process inefficiency is a known pain point. Some competitive pressure.
- Score 7-9: High opportunity cost. Manual process is a bottleneck limiting growth. Competitors are deploying agents in this domain.
- Score 10: Critical opportunity cost. Failure to agentify creates existential risk to business unit or competitive position.

AVRE™ COMPOSITE SCORING
AVRE™ Score = (ROI × 0.40) + (ROE × 0.30) + (ROF × 0.30) — adjusted by ARM™ risk penalty
Final NEV Rating:
- NEV > 7.0: HIGH VALUE — Prioritize for agentification. Build the business case now.
- NEV 5.0-7.0: MODERATE VALUE — Proceed with phased approach. Resolve capability debt first.
- NEV 3.0-5.0: CONDITIONAL VALUE — Address specific blockers before committing resources.
- NEV < 3.0: LOW VALUE — De-prioritize. Consider alternative automation approaches (RPA, API, no-code).

AVRE™ WORKED EXAMPLE: ENTERPRISE POLICY Q&A BOT
Use Case: Internal HR/IT policy Q&A agent for 10,000-employee enterprise
ROI Score: 7 — Estimated 2 hours/employee/month in policy lookup time recaptured. At $60 avg loaded hourly cost, ~$1.44M annual savings at full adoption.
ROE Score: 8 — Corpus already exists (SharePoint HR/IT docs). Minimal integration. Slack deployment is standard. Time to prototype: 2 weeks.
ROF Score: 6 — RAG corpus becomes reusable for other internal knowledge agents. Builds retrieval infrastructure. Moderate platform effect.
ARM™ Score: 3.2 (AMBER) — Low autonomy (read-only), moderate data governance (some PII in HR docs), low compliance exposure, manageable capability debt.
ARM™ Multiplier: 1.32
AVRE™ Benefit Realization: (7×0.40) + (8×0.30) + (6×0.30) = 2.8 + 2.4 + 1.8 = 7.0
Capability Debt: 3.5
NEV = 7.0 − (3.5 × 1.32) = 7.0 − 4.62 = 2.38 raw → normalized to 6.8/10 MODERATE-HIGH VALUE
